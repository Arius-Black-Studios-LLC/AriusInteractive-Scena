Content guidelines

Arleco is open to independent creators. These rules keep the catalog safe, honest, and readable for a general audience unless you label mature content clearly.

What belongs on Arleco

  • Original fiction, fan-approved transformative work you have rights to, or work you created with licensed assets.
  • Episodic visual novels and interactive fiction with clear chapter structure.
  • Stories that match their description, genre tags, and content flags.

Content flags (required when applicable)

Use studio content flags so readers know what they are opening:

  • Strong language — frequent profanity or slurs.
  • Violence — graphic or sustained depictions of harm.
  • Mature themes — sexual content, heavy horror, or other material unsuitable for minors.

Do not publish unlabeled mature content aimed at a general Discover audience.

Not allowed

  • Illegal content, exploitation of minors, or sexual content involving minors — zero tolerance.
  • Hate speech, harassment campaigns, or content primarily designed to demean protected groups.
  • Non-consensual intimate imagery or doxxing.
  • Malware, phishing, or deceptive metadata (misleading titles, cover art, or descriptions).
  • Uploading work you do not have the right to publish.

Comments & community

Readers can comment on published chapters. Keep discussion on-topic and respectful. We may remove comments or restrict accounts that spam, harass creators or other readers, or post prohibited content.

Enforcement

We may unpublish series, request edits, or suspend accounts that break these guidelines. Serious violations may be reported to appropriate authorities.

Questions before you publish? See the Help center or email help@ariusinteractive.com.
