What is a visual novel? A reader's guide

Jul 24, 2026 · For readers

A visual novel is a story you read on screen — usually with character art, dialogue, and choices that change what happens next. Think of it as a play you direct: you tap to advance lines, pick options when they appear, and unlock different scenes or endings depending on what you chose.

How reading works

Most visual novels are split into chapters (or episodes). You read one chapter at a time, make choices inside it, and return later when the creator publishes the next installment. On Arleco, everything runs in your browser — phone or desktop — with no download.

Choices, stats, and inventory

Not every line is a choice. Often you read for a while, then hit a fork: pick a dialogue option or action. Some stories track metrics (trust, courage, romance points) or key items that open or close routes later. Your save file remembers what you picked so you can replay for a different outcome.

Not the same as AI choose-your-adventure

Some apps generate endless AI stories from a prompt. Arleco is different: creators write and publish fixed chapters with designed branches, art, and pacing — closer to a indie game or serialized web novel than a chatbot. You are reading someone’s crafted plot, not an algorithm improvising.
